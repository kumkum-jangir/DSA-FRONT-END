Statistics Formulas

Measures of Central Tendency

Mean (μ) = \\( \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i}{n} \\)
Median: The middle value of a dataset (for an ordered set)
Mode: The most frequent value in a dataset

Measures of Dispersion

Variance (σ²) = \\( \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (x_i - \mu)^2}{n} \\)
Standard Deviation (σ) = \\( \sqrt{\text{Variance}} \\)
Range = \\( \text{Max} - \text{Min} \\)

Probability

Probability of an event: \\( P(E) = \frac{\text{Number of favorable outcomes}}{\text{Total number of possible outcomes}} \\)
Complement Rule: \\( P(E') = 1 - P(E) \\)
Addition Rule: \\( P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B) \\)
Multiplication Rule: \\( P(A \cap B) = P(A) \cdot P(B|A) \\)

Probability Distributions

Binomial Distribution: \\( P(X = k) = \binom{n}{k} p^k (1 - p)^{n - k} \\)
Normal Distribution: \\( P(X) = \frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2 \pi}} \cdot e^{-\frac{(X - \mu)^2}{2 \sigma^2}} \\)

Hypothesis Testing

Null Hypothesis (H₀): The hypothesis stating no effect or no difference.
Alternative Hypothesis (H₁): The hypothesis stating there is an effect or difference.
Test Statistic (z): \\( z = \frac{X - \mu}{\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}} \\)
p-value: The probability of observing a result at least as extreme as the result obtained, under the null hypothesis.

Correlation and Regression

Pearson's Correlation Coefficient: \\( r = \frac{n \sum xy - \sum x \sum y}{\sqrt{(n \sum x^2 - (\sum x)^2)(n \sum y^2 - (\sum y)^2)}} \\)
Simple Linear Regression Equation: \\( Y = a + bX \\), where a = intercept, b = slope

Confidence Interval

Confidence Interval: \\( \mu \pm Z \cdot \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} \\)
where Z is the Z-score corresponding to the confidence level.
